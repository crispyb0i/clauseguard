## Template 1: Red Flag Hook (Freelancers)

**Subject:** quick question about your contracts, [First Name]

**Body:**
Hi [First Name],

I was looking at your work on [portfolio/platform] — [one specific, genuine observation about their work, e.g. "love the branding work you did for that SaaS client"].

Quick question: when clients send you contracts, do you ever just sign them without reading every clause? Most freelancers I talk to do — not because they're careless, but because contracts are genuinely hard to parse.

I built ClauseGuard for exactly this: paste in a contract and it surfaces the red flags in plain English in about 30 seconds. Things like payment terms that let clients delay forever, IP clauses that take ownership of work you did before the project, or non-competes that are way broader than they need to be.

Free to try at clauseguard.io — no card required. Would love your honest take if you do.

[Your name]

---

**Personalization guide:**
- `[First Name]`: their first name
- `[portfolio/platform]`: where you found them (e.g. "your Behance", "Contra", "your site")
- `[one specific, genuine observation]`: one real, specific detail about their work — NOT generic. If you can't find a genuine detail, skip this lead.

**Guardrails:**
- Do not add any claims not in this template
- Do not use the words "legally", "legal advice", "legally binding", or "protects you legally"
- Do not imply ClauseGuard is a substitute for a lawyer
