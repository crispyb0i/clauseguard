Common scenarios

These clauses are in more contracts than you'd think.

Real clause patterns that appear in standard freelance and services agreements — and exactly what ClauseGuard would flag.

Freelance Designer
🔴 Non-compete · High risk
"Consultant agrees not to provide similar services to any entity operating in the same industry for 24 months following termination of this agreement."
Buried on page 7. If you sign this, you could be legally barred from working with clients in your own field for two years — even if you walk away first.
Independent Developer
🔴 IP ownership · High risk
"All work product, tools, frameworks, and methodologies used or developed during this engagement are the exclusive property of Client."
The word "used" extends ownership to your existing code libraries and reusable components — not just the deliverables you build for this client.
Consultant / Agency
🟡 Auto-renewal · Medium risk
"This agreement automatically renews for successive 12-month terms unless written notice of non-renewal is provided at least 30 days prior to the renewal date."
Miss the window by a week — because you were busy — and you're locked in for another year at the current rate, with no exit until the next cycle.

These are illustrative examples based on real clause patterns ClauseGuard is designed to detect.
